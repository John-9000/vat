Filings & compliance

Filing frequency and forms vary. This page focuses on building a clean internal process: what to track, how to reconcile, and what to double-check before submitting returns.

Monthly/quarterly routine

  • Sales ledger: invoices issued, credit notes, VAT codes, customer status.
  • Purchase ledger: supplier invoices, eligibility for input VAT, evidence stored.
  • Reconciliation: totals match accounting books and bank cashflow.
  • Evidence: export docs, intra-community supply evidence, contracts, delivery proof.

Before you file

  • Check invoice numbering continuity and required fields.
  • Confirm VAT rate selection per item/service category.
  • Review reverse-charge or special scheme transactions (if applicable).
  • Ensure adjustments/credit notes are in the correct period.
  • Keep a “file pack” for the period (PDFs, export evidence, summaries).

Add your country-specific filing forms and deadlines once you’ve confirmed them from official sources. Consider a “Last reviewed on: YYYY-MM-DD” label on this page.

Common mistakes to warn about

  • Mixing net/gross prices in reports.
  • Wrong rate category due to outdated product mapping.
  • Missing evidence for zero-rated or exempt supplies.
  • Not matching credit notes to original invoices.
