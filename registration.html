Romania VAT registration

Registration questions usually start with the facts of the model: imports, local sales, warehousing, customer type, and which entity is actually making the supply into or inside Romania.

Request assessment
1

Start with the operating model

For non-resident businesses, the VAT answer often depends more on what is happening on the ground than on a single turnover number.

2

Check imports, local stock, and local sales

Imports into Romania, local warehousing, and domestic transactions are common reasons the registration question becomes urgent.

3

Prepare the document pack

Typical work includes company documents, signatory evidence, operating descriptions, contracts, invoice samples, and logistics records where relevant.

4

Align the post-registration workflow

Registration is only the start. The invoice logic, filing inputs, and evidence retention process should be ready before activity scales further.

Typical Romania registration scenarios

  • Importing goods into Romania before local or onward sales.
  • Holding stock in a Romanian warehouse or 3PL arrangement.
  • Selling to Romanian customers through a direct or marketplace-led model.
  • Cleaning up an existing Romania sales flow that started before the VAT analysis was documented properly.

This page is general information only. Final registration decisions depend on the exact commercial and legal structure.
