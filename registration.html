VAT registration basics

Registration requirements depend on business type, turnover, activities, and cross-border transactions. This page focuses on common concepts and a practical preparation checklist.

Common situations (conceptual)

  • Local businesses: may need to register when exceeding a threshold or by opting in.
  • Cross-border supplies: can trigger registration or special reporting duties.
  • Imports/exports: VAT treatment differs from domestic supplies.
  • Digital services: special rules may apply for B2C across borders.

Preparation checklist

  • Company legal details (registration, address, representatives).
  • Business activity description (what you sell and where).
  • Estimated turnover and expected customer types (B2B / B2C).
  • Accounting setup (invoice numbering, VAT codes, reporting period tracking).
  • Bank details and proof documents typically requested.

Exact thresholds, forms, and documentation requirements can change. Link to official guidance and consider a “Talk to an accountant” CTA if you offer services.
